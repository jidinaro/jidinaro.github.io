Research

Last Working Paper Currency Depreciations, Credit Spreads and Imported Inputs
[Download PDF]

Abstract: This paper investigates how exchange rate fluctuations interact with domestic financial intermediation and production in small open economies. Using both macro and micro data, I demonstrate that depreciations are contractionary, temporarily inflationary, and reduce banksâ€™ equity, with these effects being systematically more pronounced in emerging economies compared to advanced ones. I rationalize these findings with a general-equilibrium model featuring imported-input production and bank net-worth constraints, where exchange rate movements arise endogenously. Rising imported-input costs and tighter credit conditions jointly propagate shocks, suggesting that depreciations coincide with periods of productivity weakness. Simulations show that foreign rate hikes and net-worth shocks lead to real depreciations, persistent recessions, and hump-shaped inflation dynamics, with greater openness amplifying volatility.
